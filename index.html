Research

My research lies at the frontier of computational macroeconomics, finance, and artificial intelligence. I develop deep learning algorithms and scalable computing frameworks for solving high-dimensional dynamic economic models—problems long considered computationally intractable. By breaking the curse of dimensionality, I build the tools needed to capture the true complexity of real-world economies and financial markets—and use them to answer urgent policy questions in climate economics, macro-finance, and mechanism design. My work has been published in leading academic outlets, including Econometrica, the Review of Economic Studies, the Journal of Financial Economics, and the Economic Journal, among others.
